Voice Assistant Buying Guide

Screen vs. No Screen

Smart displays with screens are better for seniors who want video calling, visual feedback, and photo slideshows. Screen-free speakers like the Echo Dot are simpler and less expensive but rely entirely on voice interaction. For most seniors, we recommend a display for easier use.

Amazon Alexa vs. Google Assistant

Both work well for seniors. Choose Amazon Echo if you shop on Amazon or want the Drop In feature for easy family check-ins. Choose Google Nest if you use Gmail, Google Photos, or prefer Google's search capabilities. Both control most smart home devices.

Setting Up for Seniors

Set up the device yourself before giving it to your parent. Pre-configure contacts, enable simplified mode (on Echo devices), and teach basic commands like "call [name]," "what time is it," and "turn on the lights." Start with 2-3 simple uses and add more over time.

Privacy Considerations

Voice assistants listen for wake words and send recordings to the cloud. You can review and delete recordings in the app settings. For bedroom use, consider the Google Nest Hub (no camera) or use the physical mute button on any device when privacy is needed.
