Smart Lighting Buying Guide

Hub vs. No Hub

Hub-based systems (like Philips Hue) are more reliable and respond faster, but cost more initially. Hubless bulbs (like LIFX and Wyze) are simpler to set up and cheaper, but depend entirely on your Wi-Fi. For seniors, we often recommend hub-based systems for their reliability.

Motion Sensors for Safety

Motion-activated lighting is one of the best investments for senior safety. Place sensors in hallways, bathrooms, and near the bed to automatically light paths at night. Set lights to turn on at 10-20% brightness so they illuminate without being blinding.

Voice Control Setup

All recommended smart lights work with Alexa and Google Assistant. Once set up, seniors can simply say "turn on the bedroom light" or "dim the living room to 50%." Create routines like "goodnight" that turn off all lights with one command.

Bulb Types and Brightness

Smart bulbs come in standard A19 (most common), BR30 (recessed cans), and specialty shapes. Check your existing fixtures before buying. For seniors, choose bulbs with at least 800 lumens for main rooms and dimmable options for nighttime use.
