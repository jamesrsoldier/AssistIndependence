Smart Security Buying Guide

Video Doorbells: Battery vs. Wired

Battery-powered doorbells are easier to install (no wiring needed) but require recharging every 1-6 months. Wired doorbells use existing doorbell wiring for continuous power. If your parent has existing doorbell wiring, wired is more convenient long-term.

Smart Locks for Caregivers

Smart locks are invaluable when caregivers, family, or emergency responders need access. You can create temporary codes that work only during certain hours, see a log of who entered and when, and lock/unlock remotely. Keep a physical key hidden outside as backup.

Professional Monitoring vs. Self-Monitoring

Professional monitoring (Ring, SimpliSafe, ADT) means a monitoring center calls emergency services if an alarm is triggered. Self-monitoring sends alerts to your phone, but you must call 911 yourself. For seniors living alone, professional monitoring provides valuable peace of mind.

Privacy Considerations

Indoor cameras can help family check on aging parents, but respect is essential. Discuss camera placement openly, avoid bedrooms and bathrooms, and give your parent control over when cameras are active. Many seniors appreciate the safety while maintaining dignity.
