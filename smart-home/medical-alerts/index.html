Medical Alert System Buying Guide

In-Home vs. Mobile Systems

In-home systems have a base station and work within your home (usually 500-1,000 ft range). They are more affordable ($20-30/month) but provide no protection outside. Mobile systems use GPS and cellular to work anywhere, ideal for active seniors ($35-50/month).

Fall Detection: Is It Worth It?

Automatic fall detection costs $10-15/month extra but can be lifesaving if you are unconscious or unable to press the button. It is not 100% accurate - some falls may not trigger it, and some non-falls might. We recommend it for seniors with balance issues, history of falls, or who live alone.

Wearable Options

Most systems offer a pendant (worn around neck) or wristband. Pendants are more common but can get in the way during activities. Wristbands stay put but may not suit all wrist sizes. Smartwatch-style options look modern but require daily charging. Choose based on what your parent will actually wear consistently.

Response Time and Monitoring

When you press the button, a trained operator answers (usually within 30-60 seconds) and assesses the situation. They can dispatch emergency services, call family contacts, or simply talk you through the situation. Look for companies with US-based monitoring centers and redundant call centers for reliability.
