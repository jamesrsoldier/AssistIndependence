Walker Buying Guide

Standard vs. Two-Wheeled Walkers

Standard walkers with no wheels provide maximum stability and are best for those with significant balance issues. Two-wheeled walkers have front wheels that glide forward while the back legs create natural braking, making them easier to use but slightly less stable.

Folding Mechanisms

Most walkers fold for storage and transport. Push-button folding is easiest for those with limited hand strength. Side-folding walkers become more compact but may require more dexterity to operate.

Height Adjustment

Proper walker height is crucial for safety and comfort. When standing with arms at your sides, the top of the walker should align with your wrist crease. Most walkers adjust from 32-39 inches to accommodate users from 5'2" to 6'4".

Accessories and Add-ons

Consider accessories like tennis ball glides for smoother sliding, carrying pouches or baskets for personal items, and padded hand grips for comfort during longer use. Platform attachments are available for those who cannot grip standard handles.
