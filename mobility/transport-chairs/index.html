Transport Chair Buying Guide

Transport Chairs vs. Wheelchairs

Transport chairs are designed to be pushed by a caregiver and have small wheels that cannot be self-propelled. They are lighter (typically 15-25 lbs) and fold more compactly than wheelchairs. Choose a transport chair if you have a caregiver and prioritize portability; choose a wheelchair if the user needs to move independently.

Weight and Materials

Aluminum frames are lightest (12-18 lbs) and best for frequent travel. Steel frames are heavier (20-30 lbs) but often more durable and affordable. Consider who will be lifting the chair - ultra-light models make a big difference for caregivers with limited strength.

Seat Width and Comfort

Standard seat widths are 17-19 inches. Measure the user's hips and add 1-2 inches for comfort. Too narrow is uncomfortable; too wide reduces lateral support. Look for padded seats and backs for extended use, and consider adding a gel cushion for longer outings.

Wheels and Maneuverability

Larger rear wheels (10-12 inches) roll more smoothly over bumps and outdoor surfaces. Smaller 8-inch wheels are fine for smooth indoor floors. Swing-away or removable footrests make transfers easier. Ensure brakes are easy to engage and hold securely.
