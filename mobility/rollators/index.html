Rollator Buying Guide

Choosing the Right Wheel Size

Wheel size is the most important factor for where you will use your rollator. Smaller 6-inch wheels are fine for indoor use on smooth floors. Medium 8-inch wheels work for indoor/outdoor use on paved surfaces. Large 10-inch or bigger wheels are best for outdoor use on uneven terrain, grass, or gravel.

Weight and Portability

If you need to lift your rollator into a car frequently, weight matters. Lightweight rollators weigh 12-16 lbs, standard models 18-22 lbs, and heavy-duty models 25+ lbs. Consider how the rollator folds too - some fold flat, others fold in half.

Seat Height and Width

Seat height typically ranges from 18-22 inches. If you are taller, look for adjustable seat heights or taller models. Seat width ranges from 12-18 inches for standard models, with bariatric models offering 20-22 inch seats.

Brakes and Safety

All rollators have loop-style hand brakes that you squeeze to slow down. Some also have parking brakes that lock the wheels when you sit. Make sure the brakes are easy to reach and require comfortable pressure to engage.
