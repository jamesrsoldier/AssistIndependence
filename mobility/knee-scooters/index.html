Knee Scooter Buying Guide

Wheel Size and Type

Wheel size determines where you can use your knee scooter. 8-inch hard rubber wheels are fine for smooth indoor surfaces. 10-12 inch pneumatic (air-filled) wheels handle outdoor surfaces and provide a smoother ride. All-terrain models have even larger wheels with suspension.

Steering and Maneuverability

Look for a tight turning radius if you will use the scooter indoors. Steerable front wheels are easier to control than fixed-wheel designs. Dual rear brakes provide better stopping power than single brakes. Test the steering before extended use.

Knee Platform

The knee platform should be well-padded with contoured foam. Platform height should adjust to keep your thigh roughly parallel to the ground. Wider platforms offer more stability but may add bulk. Memory foam padding is most comfortable for extended use.

Weight and Portability

Standard knee scooters weigh 20-25 lbs. Lightweight models are 15-18 lbs. If you need to lift the scooter into a car frequently, consider a folding model or lighter design. Some models have quick-release mechanisms for easier transport.
