Motion Light Buying Guide

Power Source Options

Hardwired lights are the most reliable and brightest, but require electrical work. Battery-powered lights install anywhere in minutes but need periodic battery changes. Solar lights are maintenance-free but depend on adequate sunlight and may dim in winter months.

Brightness and Coverage

For step and path lighting, 100-300 lumens prevents glare while providing visibility. For entry areas and porches, 300-700 lumens illuminates the space well. For security and driveway coverage, 1000-3000 lumens provides maximum visibility. Consider warm white (2700-3000K) for less harsh lighting.

Motion Sensor Features

Look for adjustable sensitivity to prevent false triggers from animals while still detecting slow movement. Adjustable duration settings of 1-10 minutes accommodate different needs. Dawn-to-dusk sensors prevent daytime activation. Some models offer always-on ambient light with brighter motion activation.

Smart Features

Smart motion lights connect to your phone for alerts, scheduling, and remote control. Some integrate with video doorbells for a complete security system. Consider whether you want smartphone notifications when motion is detected, especially useful for caregivers monitoring remotely.
