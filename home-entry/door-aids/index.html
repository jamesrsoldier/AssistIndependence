Door Aid Buying Guide

Types of Door Aids

Lever adapters clip onto existing round knobs for a quick, affordable solution. Full lever handle replacements provide a permanent upgrade. Smart locks eliminate keys entirely with codes, fingerprints, or smartphone access. Automatic door openers provide hands-free operation for maximum accessibility.

Choosing Between Manual and Smart Options

Simple lever adapters or replacement handles work for most people who just need easier grip. Smart locks add convenience with keyless entry and remote access for family caregivers. Consider your comfort with technology and whether remote monitoring is important.

Installation Considerations

Lever adapters require no tools and take seconds to install. Lever handle replacements are DIY-friendly with basic tools. Smart locks typically require the same installation as standard locks. Automatic door openers require professional installation with electrical work.

Safety and Security

Smart locks should have backup entry methods (physical key or code) in case of battery failure. Look for locks with ANSI Grade 1 or 2 ratings for security. Auto-lock features ensure doors are never accidentally left unlocked.
