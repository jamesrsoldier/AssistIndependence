Wheelchair Ramp Buying Guide

Calculating the Right Ramp Length

The ADA recommends a 1:12 slope ratio, meaning 1 foot of ramp for every inch of rise. For a 12-inch step, you need a 12-foot ramp. For power wheelchairs and scooters, a gentler 1:8 or 1:10 slope is often more comfortable and safer.

Portable vs. Permanent Ramps

Portable ramps fold for storage and travel, making them ideal for visiting different locations. Permanent or modular ramps are installed at your home for daily use and typically offer higher weight capacities and longer lengths.

Weight Capacity Considerations

Calculate the combined weight of the user, mobility device, and any cargo. Most quality ramps support 600-800 lbs. Power wheelchairs and scooters can weigh 100-300 lbs alone, so choose accordingly.

Surface and Traction

Look for ramps with non-slip surfaces that work in wet conditions. Raised edge rails prevent wheels from slipping off the sides. Some ramps have perforated surfaces to prevent water pooling.
