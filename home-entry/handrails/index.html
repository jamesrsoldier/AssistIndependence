Outdoor Handrail Buying Guide

Choosing the Right Material

Wrought iron offers the strongest grip support but is heaviest and may rust without proper coating. Aluminum is lightweight and completely rust-proof, ideal for coastal areas. Steel offers excellent strength with powder coating protection. Each material has trade-offs between weight, durability, and cost.

Mounting Options

Surface-mount rails attach to the top of steps or porches with visible base plates. Core-mount rails insert into holes drilled in concrete for a cleaner look. Wall-mount brackets attach to adjacent walls or house siding. Consider your surface type and aesthetic preferences.

Grip and Comfort

Look for handrails with 1.25 to 2-inch diameter for comfortable gripping. Round profiles are traditional, while oval or ergonomic shapes may be easier to grip for those with arthritis. Textured surfaces provide better grip in wet conditions.

Installation Considerations

Measure your steps carefully before ordering. Most handrails are adjustable to accommodate different step angles. For concrete installation, you will need a hammer drill and concrete anchors. Wood deck or porch installation typically uses lag bolts.
