Step Stool Safety Guide

Prioritize Safety Features

For seniors, safety should be the top priority. Look for step stools with non-slip surfaces, rubber feet that grip the floor, and if possible, a handrail or support handle. These features significantly reduce fall risk.

Choose the Right Height

Measure what you need to reach and choose accordingly. A 6-10 inch step stool works for most kitchen cabinets. Avoid going higher than necessary - the higher the step, the greater the fall risk. Consider reorganizing frequently-used items to lower shelves instead.

Weight Capacity Matters

Choose a step stool rated well above your body weight. Higher weight capacity indicates sturdier construction. A 300 lb rated stool will feel more stable than a 200 lb rated stool, even if you weigh much less.

Consider Storage Needs

If storage space is limited, folding step stools are convenient but make sure they have secure locking mechanisms. If you have space, non-folding models with handrails offer better stability.
