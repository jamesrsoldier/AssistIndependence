Adaptive Cutting Board Buying Guide

Key Features to Consider

For one-handed use, look for boards with spikes or prongs to anchor food while cutting. Corner guards help hold bread and sandwiches for spreading. Non-slip bases are essential for safety - choose suction cups for smooth counters or rubber feet for textured surfaces.

Spike vs Non-Spike Options

Spike boards offer the most secure hold for one-handed cutting but require careful handling. Non-spike options with textured surfaces or corner guards may be safer for those with cognitive changes but provide less secure food anchoring.

Size and Storage

Larger boards offer more work space but need more storage room. Consider where you'll store the board when not in use. Some models have wall-mount options for convenient storage.

Cleaning Considerations

Look for dishwasher-safe models when possible. Boards with removable spike plates are easier to clean thoroughly. Avoid boards with hard-to-clean crevices where food can accumulate.
