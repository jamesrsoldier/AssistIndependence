Bedside Commode Buying Guide

Types of Bedside Commodes

Standard commodes have fixed armrests and work well for most users. Drop-arm commodes allow side transfers from wheelchairs or beds. 3-in-1 commodes convert between bedside use, raised toilet seat, and toilet safety frame. Bariatric commodes support larger users with wider seats and higher weight capacities.

Seat Height and Comfort

Most commodes adjust from 16-22 inches. Taller users or those with hip or knee problems may need heights of 20-24 inches. Look for padded seats for overnight use. Open-front seats are easier to clean and more hygienic.

Cleaning and Maintenance

Removable buckets should have secure lids and splash guards. Commode liners make cleanup much easier and reduce odors. Steel frames are durable but need wiping to prevent rust. Plastic seats and armrests clean easily with disinfectant.

Stability and Safety

Check weight capacity and add 50 lbs as a safety margin. Rubber-tipped legs prevent sliding on hard floors. Wide base designs are more stable. Locking mechanisms on drop arms must engage securely before use.
