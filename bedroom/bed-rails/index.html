Bed Rail Buying Guide

Types of Bed Rails

Full-length rails run most of the bed length and prevent rolling out during sleep. Half-length rails cover part of the bed and are easier to get around. Assist rails or handles are short grab bars that help with sitting up and transfers but do not prevent falls.

Installation Methods

Under-mattress rails slide between the mattress and box spring. Frame-mounted rails clamp to the bed frame for extra stability. Freestanding rails sit on the floor and lean against the mattress. Choose based on your bed type and how much support you need.

Safety Considerations

Ensure gaps between the rail and mattress are less than 4.5 inches to prevent entrapment. Check that the rail extends high enough above the mattress (at least 13 inches recommended). Make sure the rail is rated for the user's weight plus the force of pushing against it.

Mattress Compatibility

Measure your mattress thickness before purchasing. Most rails accommodate 8-16 inch mattresses. Very thick pillow-top mattresses may need specialized rails. Adjustable beds require rails designed to move with the bed.
