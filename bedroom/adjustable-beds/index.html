Adjustable Bed Buying Guide

Semi-Electric vs. Full-Electric

Semi-electric beds have electric adjustment for head and foot positions but manual height adjustment with a crank. Full-electric beds control everything with a button. Full-electric is easier to use but costs more and requires battery backup for power outages.

Weight Capacity Considerations

Add your body weight plus the mattress weight (typically 50-150 lbs) when checking capacity. Choose a bed rated at least 100 lbs above this total for safety and longevity. Bariatric models support 600-1000+ lbs for larger users.

Key Features to Consider

Look for quiet motors (especially important for light sleepers), battery backup for emergencies, and lockable casters if you need to move the bed. Side rails, trapeze bars, and bed assist handles can be added separately for extra safety.

Mattress Compatibility

Hospital-style beds require specific mattress sizes different from standard beds. Most use 36x80 inch mattresses. Consumer adjustable bases typically use standard mattress sizes. Always verify dimensions before purchasing a mattress.
