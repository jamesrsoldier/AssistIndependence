Dressing Aids Buying Guide

Essential Dressing Aids by Need

For lower body dressing (pants, socks, shoes), you need a sock aid, long-handled shoehorn, and dressing stick. For upper body dressing (shirts, buttons), you need a button hook and possibly a dressing stick. For post-surgery recovery, a complete kit is usually the most cost-effective option.

Sock Aid Types

Flexible sock aids work well for regular socks and are easier to use. Rigid sock aids are better for compression stockings because they hold the sock open wider. Gutter-style sock aids let your foot slide in from the side, which some people find easier. Try different types to find what works for you.

Dressing Stick Features

Look for a dressing stick with an S-hook on one end (for pulling) and a push-pull hook on the other (for pushing off shoes or adjusting clothing). Length matters - 24-27 inches works for most people. Longer sticks help if you have very limited mobility.

Shoehorn Considerations

Long-handled shoehorns (18-31 inches) let you put on shoes without bending. Metal shoehorns are more durable but heavier. Plastic ones are lighter but may break with heavy use. Some have hanging loops for easy storage near your shoe area.
