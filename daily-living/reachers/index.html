Reacher Buying Guide

Choosing the Right Length

Reacher length depends on your primary use. A 26-27 inch reacher works well for picking up items within arm's reach. A 32-inch reacher is the most versatile, handling floor pickup and moderate shelf heights. A 36-inch or longer reacher is best for high cabinets or if you have significant difficulty bending.

Grip Tips and Jaw Types

Look for rubber-coated jaws for the best grip on most items. Serrated edges help with slippery objects. Magnetic tips are helpful for picking up small metal items like pins, keys, or dropped screws. Some premium reachers have rotating heads that let you grip items at any angle.

Handle and Trigger Design

If you have limited hand strength or arthritis, look for ergonomic triggers that require less pressure. Pistol-grip handles are often easier to squeeze than straight handles. Some models include a locking feature that holds items without constant squeezing.

Weight and Durability

Lightweight reachers (3-5 oz) are easier on your hands for extended use but may flex with heavier items. Heavier aluminum models (8-12 oz) are sturdier and can pick up heavier objects but may tire your hand faster. Consider having both types for different tasks.
