Recliner Lift Buying Guide

Lift Chairs vs. Portable Seat Lifts

Lift chairs are complete recliners with built-in powered mechanisms. They provide the most support and are ideal for those who need significant help standing. Portable seat lifts add lift capability to existing furniture and are more affordable but may not work for everyone. Choose based on your mobility level and budget.

Position Options

Two-position lift chairs offer upright and full recline. Three-position chairs add a TV-watching angle. Infinite position chairs let you stop at any angle and often include separate leg rest controls. More positions mean more versatility but higher cost.

Size and Fit

Measure your current seat width and height preferences. Lift chairs come in petite (for users under 5'4"), standard (5'4" to 5'10"), and tall (over 5'10") sizes. The seat depth and height should allow your feet to rest flat on the floor when seated.

Power Considerations

Most lift chairs plug into standard outlets. Look for models with battery backup so the chair works during power outages. Portable seat lifts may be spring-powered (no batteries needed) or battery-powered (more lift force but requires charging).
