Magnifier Buying Guide

Understanding Magnification Power

Magnification power tells you how much larger things appear. 2x makes items twice as big, 3x three times, and so on. Higher power means more magnification but a smaller viewing area and shorter focal distance. For general reading, 2x-3x works well. For fine print and detailed work, 5x-10x may be needed.

The Importance of Lighting

Good lighting is as important as magnification power. LED-lit magnifiers provide consistent, bright light exactly where you need it. Look for magnifiers with multiple LEDs around the lens edge and daylight-spectrum (5000-6500K) bulbs for the clearest view.

Lens Quality

Acrylic lenses are lightweight and shatter-resistant but may scratch. Glass lenses are heavier but provide clearer optics and resist scratching. Either can work well - choose based on your priorities and how you will use the magnifier.

Ergonomics and Comfort

If you use a magnifier frequently, comfort matters. Look for cushioned handles, balanced weight, and a design that does not strain your hand. For extended use, consider a stand or full-page magnifier that does not require holding.
