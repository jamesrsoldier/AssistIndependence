How to Choose the Right Walk-In Tub

Walk-in tubs are a meaningful investment in your safety and comfort. Here is what to look for so you find the right fit for your bathroom, your body, and your budget.

Step-In Threshold Height

The whole point of a walk-in tub is a safer entry, so threshold height matters a great deal. Look for a threshold of 3 to 5 inches — low enough to step over comfortably without lifting your legs high. The best models feel almost like stepping through a doorway. If you use a walker or wheelchair, look for ADA-compliant thresholds that work with transfer benches.

Door Seal & Drain Speed

Because you step in before the tub fills and must wait for it to drain before stepping out, the door seal and drain speed directly affect your daily experience. A quick-drain system can empty the tub in under two minutes, while a standard drain may take five minutes or more. Look for tubs with watertight door seals that are easy to maintain and replace over time.

Soaker vs. Whirlpool

Soaker tubs are simpler, more affordable, and have fewer parts to maintain. Whirlpool tubs add therapeutic water and air jets that can help with arthritis, muscle soreness, and circulation. The trade-off is higher cost, dedicated electrical connections, and more maintenance. If hydrotherapy is important to your daily comfort, the investment in a whirlpool model is often worthwhile.

Size & Bathroom Fit

Most walk-in tubs are designed to replace a standard 60-inch by 30-inch bathtub, but sizes vary. Measure your bathroom carefully before purchasing. Compact models like the Ariel fit spaces as small as 51 by 26 inches, while heavy-duty models may need more room. Also check that the tub can fit through your bathroom doorway during installation — some models ship in pieces for easier delivery.

Frequently Asked Questions

Walk-in tub prices typically range from $2,000 to $5,000 for the unit itself. Professional installation adds $1,000 to $3,000 depending on your bathroom layout, plumbing requirements, and whether structural changes are needed. Conversion kits like the CleanCut Step are far less expensive at $300 to $500, though professional cutting is still recommended. Budget soaker models start around $2,200 while whirlpool models with jets can run $4,500 or more.

Professional installation is strongly recommended for walk-in tubs. These units require proper plumbing connections, watertight door sealing, and often electrical work for whirlpool jets or heated seats. Improper installation can lead to leaks, water damage, or safety issues with the door seal. Conversion kits are simpler but still benefit from professional cutting to avoid damaging your existing tub. Many manufacturers offer installation services or can connect you with certified installers in your area.

A walk-in tub is a complete replacement unit with a watertight door that lets you step in through a low threshold instead of climbing over a tub wall. It fills with water for soaking and may include jets for hydrotherapy. A tub-to-shower conversion modifies your existing tub by cutting an opening in the side wall, creating a step-in entry. Conversions are much less expensive and keep your existing plumbing, but they do not provide the same soaking depth or sealed door that a full walk-in tub offers.

Most walk-in tubs take 7 to 10 minutes to fill with a standard faucet, or 4 to 6 minutes with a fast-fill faucet system. Draining typically takes 2 to 3 minutes with a standard drain and under 2 minutes with a quick-drain system. Since you must be inside the tub with the door closed before filling and wait for it to drain before opening the door, fast-fill and quick-drain features are worth prioritizing for comfort during the wait.

Standard Medicare generally does not cover walk-in tubs because they are classified as home modifications rather than durable medical equipment. However, some Medicare Advantage plans include a home safety or bathroom modification benefit that may partially cover the cost. Medicaid coverage varies by state, and some state waiver programs do fund bathroom accessibility improvements. Veterans may qualify for home modification grants through the VA. It is worth checking with your specific plan and contacting your local Area Agency on Aging for assistance programs in your area.

Most walk-in tubs are designed to fit in the same footprint as a standard 60-inch by 30-inch bathtub, making them a direct replacement in many bathrooms. Compact models like the Ariel at 51 by 26 inches can fit in smaller spaces. Before purchasing, measure your current tub alcove carefully and check the doorway width to confirm the new unit can be brought into the room. Some models offer extension panels to cover gaps if your alcove is slightly larger than the tub. A professional installer can assess your space and recommend the best fit.
