Buying Guide

What to Look for When Choosing Bathroom Safety Products

A few key considerations can make a big difference in finding the right product for your home.

Weight Capacity & Durability

Always check the rated weight capacity before purchasing. Look for stainless steel or anodized aluminum construction for long-lasting performance in wet environments. Products rated at 300 lbs or more offer a generous margin of support for most users.

Installation Method

Wall-mounted grab bars anchored into studs offer the strongest hold. Suction-cup models are a great temporary option for renters but should not be relied on as a primary support. For toilet seats and shower chairs, check that locking mechanisms are secure and tool-free.

Fit & Compatibility

Measure your space before ordering. Raised toilet seats come in standard and elongated sizes. Shower chairs need to fit inside your tub or stall with room to spare. Grab bars are available in lengths from 9 inches to 42 inches for different mounting locations.

Comfort & Ease of Use

Textured grips, padded armrests, and contoured seats make a real difference in day-to-day use. The best products feel natural and intuitive to use — you should not have to think twice about reaching for a grab bar or adjusting a shower chair.
